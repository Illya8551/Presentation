Christmas in Ukraine

Christmas in Ukraine is celebrated on January 7th (Orthodox) and December 25th (Gregorian).

Families gather on Christmas Eve for the "Holy Supper" with 12 traditional dishes.

Kutia is the main dish, symbolizing prosperity and well-being.

People sing carols and decorate their homes with a "didukh" - a sheaf of wheat.

New Year Celebration

New Year is one of the most popular and widely celebrated holidays in Ukraine.

Families decorate Christmas trees, prepare a festive table, and exchange gifts.

At midnight, it is traditional to watch the New Year's address of the President.

Streets come alive with fireworks, music, and festive celebrations.

Holiday Traditions

Ukrainian winter traditions include caroling, "shchedrivky," and nativity scene plays.

People visit neighbors and relatives, sharing festive meals and good wishes.

Sparklers, decorations, and lights create a magical festive atmosphere.

Festive Food & Sweets

Ukrainian holiday cuisine is rich, symbolic, and delicious.

Traditional dishes include borshch with pampushky, varenyky, holubtsi, fish, and uzvar.

Sweets like honey, nuts, gingerbread, and homemade pastries are essential to the celebration.
